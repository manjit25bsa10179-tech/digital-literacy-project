Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

Online payment fraud, especially through UPI, has become very common among college students in India. In this type of cybercrime, attackers trick users into sharing sensitive details or approving fraudulent payment requests.

A typical case begins when the victim receives a call, SMS, or message pretending to be from a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. The attacker creates urgency, such as claiming a problem with the victim’s account or offering a reward or refund. They then send a fake payment request or a malicious link.

The victim is asked to click the link or approve a “collect request” on their UPI app. Believing it to be genuine, the victim enters their UPI PIN. However, instead of receiving money, the amount is deducted from their account. In some cases, users are also tricked into sharing OTPs or installing screen-sharing apps, giving attackers full control.

Students and elderly people are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and in some cases, unauthorized access to bank accounts.

This highlights the importance of awareness and careful verification before making any online transaction.
